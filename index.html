About Me

Hi there! I am Sourav, a PhD student in Environmental Science and GIS at Saint Louis University. My research asks how plant phenotypic traits (e.g., leaf color, plant height, canopy structure) responds to reflected energy from the plants. I use UAV (Drone) mounted sensors to capture reflectance, point cloud, or temperature in field, and then develop computer vision and machine learning algorithms to estimate different phenotypic traits from plants. I am also interested in satellite remote sensing and geospatial statistics to study large scale plant dynamics.

Projects

  • Tassel CV

    Computer vision tools and object detection algorithms to estimate maize tassel architecture from UAV images. Utilization of YOLOv3, Faster R-CNN, and TasselNet architecture.

  • TERRA-REF

    The DoE funded project transformed plant breeding by using remote sensing to quantify plant traits. The study site consists of a large mechanical crane (Gantry Scanalyzer) with several sensors (hyperspectral, RGB, and thermal) that continuously scans crops underneath it.

  • AI for Hydrologic Connectivity
